Introduction

What Is Crochet

Crocheting is a wonderful craft where you use yarn and a hook to make (usually thick) fabric by interlocking loops. Crochet can be used to make a very wide variety of things from garments and household items, toys and small animals, and so much more. Crochet is different from knitting in that while crochet uses one hook, knitting uses two needes. Crochet is gneerally considered more versitile but knitting is considered better for garments. Crochet is also generally considered easier to learn, partially for the fact that you can fix mistakes easier and don't have to take out the whole project just because of a few dropped stitches.

Hooks

There are a lot of different types of hooks. There are wooden, metal, bamboo, plastic, and ergonomic hooks, just to name a few. Different types of hooks have their own advantages and disadvantages, and the best type of hook is all based on preference.

Metal

Pros: Metal is probably one of the most popular materials that crochet hooks come in. They are my personal favorite. Some advantages are that they are super affordable and durable, they come in a wide variety of sizes and fun colors, and glide easily which makes crocheting really fast really easy.

Cons: The only disadvantages might be that they can squeak a lot and sometimes get really sweaty. Some people also say that they can get heavy but I haven't had trouble with that.

Wood

Pros: Wood is another popular material that crochet hooks are made of. A lot of people like them because they are easy to grip, fairly lightweight, and like the natural feel. They also have more friction which is good for preventing the yarn from slipping.

Cons: Some other disadvantages is that they can be quite expensive and not as easy when it comes to maintenance.

Plastic

Pros: Plastic crochet hooks are a bit less popular but still a good choice. They are affordable and lightweight and come in a large variety of size, including vary large sizes that other crochet hook materials might not come in. Because of this, they are popular with beginners and people using really thick yarn.

Cons: Some disadvantages are that they aren't as durable or smooth as metal crochet hooks.

Ergonomic Hooks

Pros: Ergonomic crochet hooks are really cool. They are hooks that usually have metal heads and necks but they have handles that are made of materaoils such as rubber or plastic. Because of these larger handles, they are easier to grip and can help prevent strain or fatigue and cramps, allowing people to crochet for longer. Ergonomic crochet hooks also come in a wide variety of colors and styles, which is also fun.

Cons: Ergonomic crochet hooks generally cost more than a regular metal or plastic crochet hook. In addition, the handles can sometimes be too llarge or bulky for some people. Because of the way the hooks are made, sometimes the head slips in the handle and you have to superglue it back in place or something which can be annoying. These crochet hooks can also be hard to get used to for some people.

Tapered and Inline

Inline crochet hooks are hooks who's shaft is the same size as the head. They do not taper down at the neck. Tapered crochet hooks are hooks that do get narrower at the neck. Some people say that the inline hooks are better for precision and tapered hooks are better for speed, but whether inline or tapered crochet hooks are better is based on personal preference and what feels most comfortable for you.

Hook Sizes

Crochet hooks come in lots of different sizes. They come in sizes that go as small as steel hooks that are less than a milimeter to hooks made from other materials that can get up to 30 millimeters. Hooks are a big part of crochet. A lot depends on them. A smaller hook with a certain size yarn will make fabric that is thick and holds its shape a bit better. A larger hook with the same size yarn will create a loose, more flexible fabric that might have some holes in it. What hook size you use is a lot to do with what will work best for the particular project and yarn, gauge, and personal preference.

Yarn

Acrylic

Pros: Acrylic is a really popular yarn. It is widely available n a wide variety of colors, sizes and textures pretty much anywhere you can get yarn. It is really affordable, it can be quite durable, and most acrylic is machine washable making it super easy to care for. It is also hypoallergenic so it won't bother people with allergies to things like wool.

Cons Though there is a lot to love about acrylic, there are some disadvantages. Acrylic doesn't provide the same kind of warmth that other fibers like wool do. It doesn't allow for much breathability, it isn't the most environmentally friendly yarn, and it can be a danger around lots of heat because it is made of plastic.

Wool

Pros: Wool is another classic fiber. It is known for being really insulating and keeps moisture away from the body, it can be quite durable and is a natural fiber so it is biodegradable, and it is fairly versatile .

Cons: There are quite a few disadvantages to wool. Wool is generally more expensive than yarns like acrylic, the colors aren't as vibrant, they are harder to care for because they can't be machine washed because they might felt, and they can attract pests like moths.

Cotton

Pros: Cotton is another popular fiber. Cotton is breathable, generally soft and comfortable, making it great for clothes and garments. It also soaks up moisture pretty well, which makes it good for things like dishcloths and other things for around the house. Cotton is also strong and hypoallergenic. A couple final advantages are that the stitches hold their shape making it good for projects that need to hold their shape. Cotton is also easy to care for because it is machine washable.

Cons: Cotton doesn't have a whole lot of cons. However, it doesn't have a lot of stretch which affects shape, obsorbing water can make it heavy, cotton's stiffness can make it hard to work with, and colors can bleed.

Silk

Pros: Silk is another good type of yarn. It is known for its strengh and drape. It is also really smooothe, and temperature regulating, keeping you warm in cold weather and cool in warm weather.

Cons: Silk has some disadvantages such as its price, its slipperiness making it hard to work with, its tendancy to get weaker when wet, and the fact that it can be difficult to work with are some things to consider when buying wool.

Nylon

Pros: A less common but still popular fiber, nylon is known for being one of the strongest manmade fibers. It is lightweight, dries fast, and holds its shape really well, even after being stretched.

Cons: Some disadvantages of nylon are its environmental impact, heat sensitivity, the fact that it doesn't absorb water, and it being prone to staining.

Other Yarns

Apart from acrylic, wool, cotton, silk and nylon, there are other types of yarn such as polyester, mohair, and bamboo, which are other fibers that are a bit less common but still pretty well known and used auite a bit. Another thing that is pretty common is blends, which are when there is yarn with multiple different fibers. One common example of this is acrylic and wool being put together, which can be really good because it combines the warmth and texture of wool but the affordability and easy care of acrylic.

Another thing to keep in mind is yarn sizes. There are quite a few different yarn sizes. First there is size 1 (super fine or fingering weight) which is the smallest yarn after lace, and is commonly used for socks and things for babies. Size 2 (sport weight) is the yarn a bit larger than that and is commonly used for lightweight garments and baby clothes. Size 3 yarn (DK or light worsted weight) is one of the more common, versitile yarns and is used for a lot of different things. Size 4 (worsted weight) which is the most popular and one of the most versitile yarn weights can be used for prety much almost anything. Size 5, 6, and 7 yarns (bulky, super bulky, and jumbo) is a bit bigger and is commonly used for accessories, garments, and anything you want to work up really quickly.

Introduction to Patterns

Patterns are written instructions that tell you how to make something. There are a lot of different places (such as websites) where you can get crochet patterns. From large yarn companies' patterns to smaller creators who share their patterns, there is a lot. And some websites and places are better or easier to access than others. And some patterns are way easier to read than others. I am going to share 2 of my favorite places to get patterns, which one I prefer and why I like it.

Yarnspirations

This is by far my favorite websites to get patterns. Yarnspirations is a commpany that makes yarns such as Red Heart, Bernet, and Caron. There are a lot of things I love about it. It allows you to apply filters so you can choose your craft, yarn size, or whether you want to see patterns or yarn. There are a lot of patterns. Each oone tells you which yarn brand it uses such as "Red Heart Ripples in the Pond Throw". The patterns are Really easy to follow and I never have any troubble figuring out what they are telling me to do. In addition, some of the patterns show diagrams of the stitches in the patterns, which can be very helpful if you want a more visual thing to go along with the written directions. Possibly my favorite part about the website is that we don't need to sign up to the website or enter your email to be able to access the patterns. The only disadvantage is that every single time I go to the website it asks me what country I'm from for shipping even though I've answered that question so many times before.

LionBrand

Lionbrand is another good website to get patterns from. I personally do not use it. It is another website that lets you filter content to select what craft or yarn size you want. The patterns are easy to follow and free to get. LionBrand also provides crochet kits and knit kits that come with what you need to complete a project. There is only one minor disadvantage which is the reason I don't get patterns from this website. Though the patterns are free, you have to sign up to the website with your email. Once you do this, Lionbrand will send you a trillion emails per week with nothing but notifications about yarn deas. And even if you ask to sign out, you will still get those yarn deal emails.

Learning Crochet

Where To Learn

There are a lot of different ways that you can learn crochet. One way you can learn is with books. Some books give you step by step instructions on how to do different stitches with images. Another way you can learn is classes. Some places, like local yarn stores, offer in person crochet classes to teach you. Finally, one of the ways that helped me learn, is videos. On various sites (including YouTube) have lots and lots of videos showing you how to do pretty much any stitch imaginable.
